Methodology

This application generates maps of Israel's virtual water trade by combining data from two sources.  One source provides the total quantity of each commodity exchanged between Israel and each of its trading partners. The other source provides the average virtual water footprint of commodities produced in each country that Israel trades with.  

Sources

UNESCO-IHE: Institute for Water Education

Mekonnen, M.M. and Hoekstra, A.Y. (2011) National water footprint accounts: the green, blue and grey water footprint of production and consumption, Value of Water Research Report Series No. 50, UNESCO-IHE, Delft, the Netherlands.

Appendix XI of this report provides data about the virtual water costs of producing different commodities in each country. Each commodity has different virtual water costs and each country produces commodities at different virtual water costs. This dataset captures these differences and is clarified in Volume 1 of the National Water Footprint Accounts report. This dataset can be used to establish the per-unit virtual water costs of Israel's trading of 90 different commodities, such as meats and tomatoes.

International Trade Centre

"Bilateral Trade." Table. Trade Map: Trade Statistics for International Business Development. International Trade Centre, n.d. Web. 21 Oct. 2013. <http://www.trademap.org>.

This database provides detailed import and export information for each country, broken down into categories such as oils. It will be used to research Israel's recent trading patterns with other countries. It can be used to determine what Israel is trading with other countries and the quantities of each commodity that Israel is exchanging.
