About

This application generates animated maps of Israel's virtual water trade.

Virtual water trade is the hidden flow of water if food or other commodities are traded from one place to another. For instance, it takes 1,600 cubic meters of water on average to produce one metric tonne of wheat.  When a country like Israel imports a tonne of wheat, it indirectly imports 1,600 cubic meters of water on average.  The precise volume of water can be more or less depending on climatic conditions and agricultural practice in the origin country.

Israel is a country stressed for water.  This mostly-arid country has the fourth highest population density in the world.  Its major water sources are under overwhelming demand by Israelis and Palestinians.  Israel is in need of alternative water sources.  Importing virtual water from other countries might be part of the solution.

Israel's Virtual Water Trade Map was developed by Raphael Astrow, a student in Carnegie Mellon University's Information Systems Program, under direction by Dr. Randy Weinberg, Program Director.
