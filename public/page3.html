About

This application shows how much water Israel imports and exports from other countries using an animated map.

On the map, the bigger the dot is on a country, the more virtual water that country is exporting. A table appears below the map to demonstrate how the map's data is calculated. The standard unit of measurement for virtual water trade is million cubic meters (mcm).

The application also demonstrates the effects of third party intermediaries on Israel’s virtual water trade. For example in 2012, Israel imported 675,452,356 cubic meters of green virtual water from Switzerland. However, Switzerland does not itself produce large amounts of wheat for export; third party import / export firms located in Switzerland act as intermediaries in Israel’s global wheat trade.

Virtual water trade is the hidden flow of water when food or other commodities are traded from one place to another. For instance, it takes 1,600 cubic meters of water on average to produce one metric tonne of wheat.  When a country like Israel imports a tonne of wheat, it indirectly imports 1,600 cubic meters of water on average.  The precise volume of water can be more or less depending on climatic conditions and agricultural practice in the origin country.

Israel is a country stressed for water.  This mostly-arid country has the fourth highest population density in the world.  Its major water sources are under demand by Israelis and Palestinians. Israel imports virtual water from other countries to help balance its water needs.

Israel's Virtual Water Trade Map was developed by Raphael Astrow, a student in Carnegie Mellon University's Information Systems Program, under direction of Dr. Randy Weinberg, Program Director.
